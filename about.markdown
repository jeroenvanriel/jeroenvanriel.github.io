This site is a collection of technical notes, expository writing, and documentation of personal projects. It serves as a place where I write about what I build and what I learn in the process.

I hold a dual Master’s degree in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science from Eindhoven University of Technology.
Courses in these programs covered a wide range of topics, including algorithms, data structures, machine learning, optimization, numerical methods, and more. I also completed a [thesis project](https://github.com/jeroenvanriel/traffic-scheduling) on using deep reinforcement learning for optimizing traffic flow at autonomous intersections.

During my thesis project, I realized how little experience I had with technical writing beyond writing assignment reports, involving the construction of some relatively small mathematical models and presenting formulae. This gave me a renewed appreciation for the quality of scientific and technical literature that I had previously only been consuming and inspired me to practice writing about technical topics myself.
