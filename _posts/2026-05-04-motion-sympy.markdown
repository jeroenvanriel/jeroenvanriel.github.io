As part of my Master's thesis project, I ventured a little bit into classical control theory, trying to apply Pontryagin's maximum principle to derive a fast algebraic alternative to an existing direct transcription procedure.

## The motivation

Motion planning procedure.

## My challenges

Classical optimal control theory has always felt interesting, because it seems so highly applicable in practise.
This is also due to the fact that it may be understood as eventually inspiring the popular reinforcement learning approach, for which there is arguable more accessible material available (manifested in the famous book by Sutton and Barto).

For me, the central focus was to understand Pontryagin's maximum principle.

I can apply the maximum principle as it is, but I lack the necessary background and depth of understanding to apply it for higher-order state constraints.
